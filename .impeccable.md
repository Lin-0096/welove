## Design Context

### Users
Both locals living in Finnish cities (Helsinki, Oulu) and tourists/visitors. Locals use it to discover hidden gems and stay updated on the best spots; tourists need fast, reliable recommendations from a source they can trust. Context is typically mobile, on-the-go, often hungry or looking for somewhere to go right now.

### Brand Personality
**Three words**: Warm · Opinionated · Local

welove is the friend who actually knows the city — not a faceless algorithm, not a tourist trap guide. It speaks with confidence ("The best cafés in Helsinki") and warmth. It's curated, not exhaustive. It has a point of view.

Emotional goal: users should feel *in the know* — like they're getting a tip from an insider, not just search results.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Direction**: Editorial food media — Eater/Infatuation meets Finnish editorial restraint.

- **Typography**: Bold serif display font for headings (uppercase, editorial authority), clean humanist sans for body. Strong typographic hierarchy is the primary design element.
- **Color palette**: Warm cream background, near-black ink. Minimal accent — maybe a single warm red or amber only for key interactive states. No gradients, no dark mode as primary.
- **Layout**: Left-aligned, asymmetric. Place names in uppercase, large. Rank numbers (#1, #2) as structural dividers, not decorative. Horizontal rules as separators — editorial, not cards.
- **Tone**: Less "app", more "print magazine that happens to be digital". Confident, stripped-back, typographically rich.

**References**: Eater, The Infatuation, early Monocle digital.

**Anti-references**: Generic shadcn card grid, glassmorphism, AI slop palette (cyan/purple/neon), dashboard metrics layout, centered hero with gradient text.

### Design Principles
1. **Typography does the heavy lifting** — hierarchy, weight, and size communicate importance; avoid decorative elements that compete with content.
2. **Opinionated, not neutral** — welove has a point of view; design choices should feel intentional and editorial, not safe and generic.
3. **Mobile-first, content-first** — the interface serves the content; chrome is minimal, every pixel earns its place.
4. **Warm but not cutesy** — friendly tone without soft pastels or playful illustrations; warmth comes from typography and texture, not color or icons.
5. **Trust through restraint** — fewer recommendations, displayed with confidence, feel more trustworthy than exhaustive lists with visual noise.
